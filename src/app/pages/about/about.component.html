About Us

H. A. Dharmasena began making guitars when he was a teenager. Now, the legacy of his craftsmanship lives on through his grandson, Bhagya Madhushan, who runs the family business. Guitar making is deeply rooted in their family history, with Mr. Dharmasena passing on his passion and expertise to his son, Kithsiri Hathurusinghe, and later to his grandson, Bhagya Madhushan. From their modest home in Kolonnawa, three generations of guitar makers have crafted and sold guitars all over the country. However, the journey has not been without its challenges. Mr. Dharmasena often lamented lost opportunities and the lack of appreciation for their craft in the face of competition from cheap imports, particularly from China and India. Despite the obstacles, Mr. Dharmasena remained dedicated to his craft, recalling the better times when his guitars were sold all over the country during the 60s and 70s. He took pride in the quality of their products, which extended beyond guitars to include violins as well. However, with the influx of cheap Chinese products, they had to discontinue making violins, preserving only a few as souvenirs. Bhagya Madhushan, now at the helm of the family business, continues the tradition of handcrafting guitars in their spacious workshop. Despite the challenges faced by the industry, he remains optimistic about the future, carrying forward the legacy of his grandfather and father. The struggles faced by the Dharmasena family are emblematic of the broader challenges encountered by traditional craftsmen in the modern era. Despite the odds, their dedication and perseverance serve as an inspiration to artisans and craftsmen worldwide. As Bhagya Madhushan continues to steer the family business forward, the legacy of Dharmasena's craftsmanship lives on, a testament to the enduring spirit of artisanal craftsmanship.
